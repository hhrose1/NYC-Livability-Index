How to Check HPD Violations for NYC Apartments

1

Search for Violations

Go to HPD Online and check three tabs:

  • Complaints
  • Violations
  • Bedbug Reports
2

Assess Violation Severity

Review violations by class (A/B/C) and check:

  • How many are open vs. closed?
  • How recent are they?
  • What codes are they?
3

Something Looks Suspicious

If you see no violations at all or something seems off:

  • Look up the lot and block number in the NYC Open Data HPD Violations dataset
  • Cross-reference to catch missing or unreported violations
4

Research the Landlord

Look up the owner in the HPD database, then:

  • Google the owner's name — are there lawsuits? News articles?
  • Google the property management company — check reviews and complaints
  • Read what people actually say, not just star ratings

Key Tips

Decoding Violation Codes

The HPD code is the number directly in front of the § symbol. Search codes in the table below.

Reading Between the Lines

Check the Complaints tab — multiple complaints but no violations could mean overly thorough neighbors. Age matters more than quantity — violations from 2001 matter less than a single Class C violation in 2025.

Violation Severity

Class A (minor): A few are normal for older buildings
Class B (hazardous): Multiple open = yellow flag
Class C (immediately hazardous): ANY open = red flag

Critical Red Flags

Codes 729/730: Building chronically neglected
Codes 759/760/765: Vacate orders — illegal if rented

Quick Reference
